Disciple-Making Pathway

Categories of Growth

A synthesis of elder input on what a disciple needs at each stage of growth

Team — thank you for the time and care you put into the input form.

What follows is a synthesis of your responses. For each category of growth, I've pulled together the themes where there was clear agreement among us, included a sample of your own words, and offered an early read on what this might mean for the pathway. This is a working document — a first look at where our collective wisdom is converging.

Read it with the people you lead in mind. Where does this ring true? Where is something missing?

Observation & Imitation · Establishing

New Disciple

Establishing a new disciple in their faith through presence, modeling, and guided participation.

Gospel Story · Foundation

Grounded in the gospel and established in their new identity in Christ.

Where we agreed
New identity in Christ The weight & gift of grace Repentance leading to joy Understanding their own story God's redemptive arc Confession & forgiveness
In your words
SamThey need to know the weight of Grace and how that plays out in life. The need to experience repentance (sorrow) that leads to joy. The need to know how to unpack their story, God's work in their life.
JoshuaBe able to define their new identity in Christ as they learn how and why the gospel has transformed their life.
MarkKnow the essentials — sin, salvation is a gift, Christ died for you, confess and believe. Experience the joy of the Lord. A foundational understanding of their new identity (Eph. 1).
TimBiblical theology — God's saving acts through history. Seeing history from God's perspective: Creation, Fall, Redemption, Restoration.
Pathway implication

Strong consensus: a New Disciple needs an experiential grounding in grace — not just information, but the felt weight of forgiveness, repentance, and their new identity. Pathway content should prioritize who they now are before what they should do.

Life in Community · Foundation

Established into biblical community and learning what it means to belong to a spiritual family.

Where we agreed
Experiencing genuine care & belonging Community is not optional Learning to be vulnerable Conflict as growth Safe spaces to share Seeing others model it
In your words
SamThe church is a body, conflict is inevitable, growth opportunities abound. Community is a must. We were not meant to walk alone.
JoshuaBegin learning how to be reliant, vulnerable and engaged with their new brothers and sisters in Christ.
MarkExperience what it is like to be cared for and loved genuinely by the church. Growing confidence that they can depend on those in community with them.
Pathway implication

Unified read: the goal at this stage is belonging before contributing. A New Disciple needs to be received, cared for, and shown what healthy community looks like — not yet asked to lead or serve. Getting them connected to a group quickly is essential.

Participation & Formation · Growing

Growing Disciple

Growing a young disciple through practice, guidance, and increasing personal responsibility.

Gospel Story · 101

Establishing a foundational grasp of the gospel and how it transforms their story and purpose.

Where we agreed
Articulating their grace story Identity shaping daily life Gospel touches everyday life The meta-narrative of Scripture Looking "different" — and okay with it Marketplace integration
In your words
SamThey need to know the Truth, the Word, the Bible. Vocational integration — how to take the Gospel into their career or marketplace.
JoshuaAble to articulate how the gospel has shaped their new identity so they can share their grace story with someone.
MarkHow their new identity shapes the way they make decisions. What it looks like to feel "different" than their unbelieving friends — and be okay with it.
Pathway implication

The gospel moves from received to expressed. We want Growing Disciples to tell their story and begin applying gospel logic to real life — work, relationships, decisions.

Knowing God · 101

Establishing a base of theological clarity that shapes their view of God, themselves, and the world.

Where we agreed
Character of God (Trinity, grace, mercy) Basic doctrines of the faith A biblical lens for the world Scripture sufficient for all of life The Spirit illuminating Scripture Confident personal Bible reading
In your words
SamCharacter of God, the Trinity, what Grace and Mercy look like in the life of the believer.
JoshuaHow God's word is sufficient for all of life's circumstances. How they can see the world through a biblical lens.
TimIntroduction to main doctrines: Bibliology, Christology, Soteriology, Anthropology, and theology proper.
MarkBegin to understand the meta-narrative of Scripture. Growing in basic doctrinal things: baptism, communion, trinity.
Pathway implication

Clear alignment: a Growing Disciple needs theological grounding, not theological depth. Focus on who God is and key doctrines that form a framework. Tim's doctrinal list and Mark's nod to Summit's belief statements give us a practical starting point.

Communion with God · 101

Helping them grow in their relationship with God by forming transformative spiritual habits.

Where we agreed
How to read the Bible personally Developing a prayer life Dependence on the Holy Spirit Joy of confessing sin Fruit of the disciplines Heart-level surrender
In your words
JoshuaThey need to know how to read their Bible and how to talk with God. Seeing the fruit of spiritual disciplines and pointing others to them.
SamThe voice of the biblical Shepherd, heart level surrender, silence and solitude, biblical definition of abiding and delight.
MarkUnderstand why they need to repent even as Christians. How habits shape Christian character. The joy that comes from confessing sin and walking in the light.
Pathway implication

We want Growing Disciples to move from knowing about prayer and Scripture to doing it personally. Content here should be practical and formative — actual tools, real experience of God.

Life in Community · 101

Learning to participate in the body by building relationships, discovering their gifts, and beginning to serve.

Where we agreed
Community is necessary for growth Accountability & honesty Beginning to serve The economics of grace Discovering gifts in context Healthy group rhythms
In your words
SamThe economics of grace, accountability, the healing power of confession, and culture-making rhythms played out in Christian circles.
JoshuaEngaged in joyful service in their community, learning to appreciate and value the body of Christ.
MarkReally believe community is necessary. Experience others speaking into their life in love — encouraged, rebuked, admonished. Begin serving alongside others.
Pathway implication

The shift from Infant: now they're not just receiving community — they're participating. We want Growing Disciples to experience real accountability and begin serving, not just attending.

Life on Mission · 101

Beginning to live with increasing confidence and intentionality where they live, work, and play.

Where we agreed
A biblical basis for everyday placement Gospel shaping relationships Confidence to share — with support OK not having all the answers Standing firm at work Seeing the gospel transform others
In your words
SamMost don't work for the church and never will. They need the biblical background of everyday placement. They need to be OK not having all the answers.
JoshuaThe gospel is beginning to shape how they interact with their friends, family and peers.
MarkArticulate the gospel with support around them to step in if needed. Stand firm in their workplaces when they encounter peer pressure.
Pathway implication

A recurring theme: everyday life is the mission field. At this stage the goal isn't polished evangelism — it's awareness and growing confidence. Give them language for their normal life, not a program to join.

Ownership & Transformational Practice · Equipping

Maturing Disciple

Equipping a maturing disciple through challenge, responsibility, and guided leadership of others.

Gospel Story · 201

Learning to apply the gospel to all of life, articulate it clearly, and live within God's redemptive mission with growing clarity and purpose.

Where we agreed
Refuting lies with gospel truth Never graduating from the basics Helping others apply the gospel Gospel-centered leadership Settled confidence in their identity Apologetics & grace story
In your words
MarkHow to refute the lies the devil and their heart tell them. Ability to clearly articulate God's plan of redemption, how they fit into it, why the church matters.
JoshuaA thorough understanding of the gospel and their grace story — can help others apply the gospel to their life.
SamClarity, Narrative, Heart, and Gospel-Centered Leadership.
TimTraining in sharing one's grace story and apologetics. A greater sense that their salvation is all grace.
Pathway implication

The gospel becomes a weapon and a lens, not just a foundation. We want Maturing Disciples to apply it internally (against lies and sin) and externally (helping others). Content should be applied and relational.

Knowing God · 201

Deepening theological understanding so truth increasingly shapes their experience, identity, and engagement with the world.

Where we agreed
Sound doctrine — not tossed around Orthodoxy shaping orthopraxy Identify & engage spiritual warfare Summit belief statements as anchor Decisions from biblical conviction Deeper systematic theology
In your words
MarkGrounded in sound doctrine — not easily tossed to and fro. Know Summit's "Our Beliefs" statements and articulate why they personally believe them. Able to identify and engage in spiritual warfare.
JoshuaSee deeper theological truths. Making life decisions in light of biblical conviction rather than worldly aspirations.
TimHistorical theology — awareness of doctrines and controversies found in creeds and councils since the 1st century.
Pathway implication

We want Maturing Disciples to move from knowing doctrine to being shaped by it. Mark's reference to Summit's belief statements is worth taking seriously as a curriculum anchor here. Spiritual warfare also surfaced as a distinctive need.

Communion with God · 201

Learning to abide deeply with God through mature spiritual disciplines that shape a life of ongoing obedience and surrender.

Where we agreed
Confident in the disciplines Leading others in study & prayer A felt intimacy with God Comfortable in silence Discerning the Spirit's fruit in others When God "feels" distant
In your words
JoshuaA disciplined experience of God through prayer and Scripture with ability to lead others in Bible study and prayer. A felt sense of intimacy with God.
SamBeing OK in the quiet and stillness. Prayer and silence, relational rest, listening skills, and contentment with God alone.
MarkHow to confidently practice the spiritual disciplines. Ability to discern fruit of the Spirit in others. How to respond when God "feels" distant.
Pathway implication

The shift here is significant: from forming habits to leading others in them. A Maturing Disciple should be able to invite others into their prayer life, not just maintain one.

Life in Community · 201

Learning to live in accountable, intentional relationships while beginning to take responsibility for the spiritual growth of others.

Where we agreed
Helping others fight sin Understanding church structure & loyalty Beginning to dream about leading Walking through hard things together Shepherding rhythms Knowing when to refer for counseling
In your words
MarkHow to help others within their community fight sin. What it means to fight beside others. Beginning to dream about leading community.
SamKnow the overall structure of the local church, biblical loyalty, the ability to point others to counseling when needed, understanding of shared suffering.
JoshuaInitiating and pursuing community — having a vision for future leadership, aware of the dangers of an absence of community.
Pathway implication

Community shifts from experienced to stewarded. Maturing Disciples should be moving toward leadership — not just participating, but beginning to carry others. Practical shepherding skills emerge as a key need.

Life on Mission · 201

Taking intentional ownership of their personal mission — actively engaging the people and places God has positioned them in with gospel clarity and purpose.

Where we agreed
Knowing who they're on mission to Perseverance through opposition Demonstrating mission to others Sharing clearly without a safety net Salt & light in community Patience in mission
In your words
MarkWho is in their circle and where they are spiritually. Experience attacks on mission, but perseverance through God's grace. Begin demonstrating how to do this to others.
JoshuaSharing the gospel in the places they live, work and play. Able to clearly share the gospel with those they come in contact with.
SamThey need to know patience, be in community, know the Gospel story, and practice their own biblical leadership benchmarks at work and home.
Pathway implication

Mission becomes owned and intentional. Knowing who you're on mission to is a practical anchor several of us named. Pathway content should help Maturing Disciples name their mission field and engage it without always needing others present.

Modeling & Multiplication · Training

Multiplying Disciple

Training leaders to make disciples through a life worth imitating and intentional investment in others.

Leading Others · 301 · All Categories

Equipped to shepherd, teach, and reproduce — investing in others so they too can make disciples.

Where we agreed
Know their gifts, style & limits Assist → Watch → Learn → Do Accountability beyond those they lead Christ-like communication & EQ Heart dynamics & idols Teaching others to study the Bible Lament & guiding others in it Spiritual warfare at a leadership level Conflict resolution & change Listening — slow to speak Sitting with people in pain Defined training pipelines Flexibility & adaptability
In your words
SamThey need to know their HEART and HEAD. Both. Their gifts, leadership style, and limitations. They need to Assist, Watch, Learn, and Do. Leadership changes — be flexible, try new ways, and try again. Listen to God.
JoshuaLead others in Bible study and prayer. Teach others to share and apply the gospel. Facilitate community. A simple frame: Infant → See. Child → Learn. Young Adult → Do. Adult → Teach.
MarkHeart dynamics — helping people identify their own heart idols. Lament. Doctrinal clarity — able to teach with clarity. Slow to speak, quick to listen. Comfortable sitting with people walking through hard things.
TimChrist-like communication, strategic thinking, emotional intelligence, change management, active listening, spiritual discernment, conflict resolution, adaptability. Specific pipelines for CG leaders, ministry leads, deacons, elders.
Pathway implication

This stage drew our richest and most varied input — which makes sense. Three threads emerge: self-knowledge (gifts, limits, style), relational skill (listening, sitting with pain, conflict), and reproducibility (able to train others in what they themselves do). Joshua's See → Learn → Do → Teach framing is worth exploring as a simple way to communicate the whole pathway.

A Few Things I'm Noticing

  • Identity before activity. Across every stage, we keep returning to who a person is in Christ before what they should do. That instinct is worth protecting as we build.
  • A clear arc of reproduction. Receiving → participating → leading → reproducing. The progression from stage to stage is remarkably consistent across all of our responses.
  • See · Learn · Do · Teach. Joshua offered this as a simple way to name each stage. It may be the clearest language we have for explaining the pathway to the whole church.
  • One gap to decide together. Our form didn't ask about Communion with God at the New Disciple stage, so we have no input there yet. Worth deciding whether that category belongs at this stage or if our current structure is right.
